Introduction to Chemistry

Atomic Structure

Chemistry is the study of matter and its interactions. Atoms are the building blocks of matter. The structure of an atom consists of protons, neutrons, and electrons. The formula for calculating the number of neutrons is:

Neutrons = Atomic Mass - Atomic Number

Chemical Formulas

Chemical compounds are represented by chemical formulas. For example, water is written as H2O and carbon dioxide as CO2. These notations help us understand the composition of compounds.

Periodic Table

The periodic table is an arrangement of chemical elements in a tabular format. Each element is placed in a specific location based on its atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties. For example, Hydrogen (H) has an atomic number of 1.

Acids and Bases

An acid is a substance that donates a hydrogen ion (H+) to another substance. A base, on the other hand, accepts a hydrogen ion. The strength of acids and bases is measured using the pH scale.

The pH of water is 7, indicating it's neutral, while vinegar has a pH of around 3, making it acidic.
